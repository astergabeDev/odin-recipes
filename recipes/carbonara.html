Spaghetti Carbonara

Description

A classic Roman dish, Spaghetti Carbonara is made with eggs, cheese (Pecorino Romano), pancetta, and black pepper. Simple and rich, this dish combines creamy, peppery, and savory flavors for a truly delightful pasta experience.

Ingredients

  • 400g spaghetti
  • 100g pancetta or guanciale, diced
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 cup prato cheese
  • Salt, to taste

Steps

  1. Start by boiling a large pot of salted water. Add the spaghetti and cook until al dente, following the package instructions.
  2. While the spaghetti is cooking, heat the olive oil or butter in a large pan over medium heat. Add the diced pancetta or guanciale and fry until it's crispy.
  3. Add minced garlic to the pan and sauté for about 1 minute, until fragrant.
  4. In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, Pecorino Romano cheese, and black peppe
  5. Once the spaghetti is done, reserve 1 cup of pasta water and then drain the rest.
  6. Add the drained spaghetti to the pan with pancetta/guanciale and toss to combine.
  7. Remove the pan from heat and quickly pour in the egg mixture, stirring quickly and ensuring the eggs don’t scramble. Add reserved pasta water a little at a time until you get the desired sauce consistency.
  8. Serve immediately, garnishing with additional Pecorino Romano and a sprinkle of black pepper.
